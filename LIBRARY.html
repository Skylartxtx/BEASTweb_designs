FOUR DIVINE BEAST

God of the East-青龙

(Qing Long) - Azure Dragon

God of the West-白虎

(Bai Hu) - White Tiger

God of the South-朱雀

(Zhu Que) - Vermilion Bird or Phoenix

God of the North-玄武

(Xuan Wu) - Black Tortoise or Black Warrior

Introduction:

The Four Great Divine Beasts are the mythical beasts of ancient Chinese legend, namely Qinglong, Baihu, Zhuque, and Xuanwu, which belong to the combination of ancient mythology and astronomy.The image of the Four Spirits was roughly formed during the Warring States period, and the motivation for the worship of the Four Spirits was because people believed that they could bring auspiciousness.They originated from primitive Nature worship and are the combination of animal gods and astronomical gods.After the birth of Taoism, the Four Spirits were regarded as Taoist protectors, further strengthening their position in Chinese culture.
